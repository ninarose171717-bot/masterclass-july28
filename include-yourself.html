A four-month private journey with Nina Rose

Include yourself in your own life.

A four-month private coaching journey for the woman who is done leaving herself out of the life she built.

One-to-one  ·  Four months  ·  A small number of spaces

Begin the conversation

Look at your life for a moment.

The clothes you actually wear, instead of the ones you want to wear.

The apartment that never quite feels like you.

The situationship you keep entertaining, when what you long for is marriage.

The friendships you keep warm, with people who are never quite there.

The yes you say before you’ve even checked in with yourself.

None of this is random. And none of it means something is wrong with you.

Your life has been faithfully following an old map. One you never chose on purpose.

Before we ask what you want, we look at something quieter: what has been deciding for you.

  • Avoiding rejection.
  • Being needed.
  • Staying safe.
  • Never disappointing anyone.
  • Not taking up too much space.
  • Quietly proving that you’re enough.

Once you see it, the small things start to make sense.

Oh … that’s why I keep saying yes.

That’s why my home never feels like me.

That’s why I keep choosing people who can’t quite reach me.

We’re not changing who you are. We’re discovering what has been speaking louder than you.

I want to be honest about what this journey is, because it isn’t about becoming confident, magnetic, healed, or new.

Life doesn’t necessarily become easier. You simply stop facing it alone.

No matter what life brings, I won’t abandon myself there.

That sentence, lived daily, is the whole work.

The journey

Four months. Four movements.

Month one

Meeting yourself

Not fixing yourself. Not healing yourself. Meeting yourself. We look at who you are and what your life has quietly been organized around, with honesty, and with kindness for how it got this way.

Month two

Staying with yourself

The heart of the work. Learning to feel what’s alive in you, your needs, fear, desire, tenderness, anger, grief, pleasure, without leaving.

Stress says: I’ve lost myself. Pressure says: I’m still here. This month builds the capacity to stay.

Month three

Standing by yourself

The relationship becomes visible. Boundaries, honesty, desire. Saying what’s true instead of going quiet and inward. Receiving a no without making it mean something about your worth.

An open heart, with discernment about who has access to it.

Month four

Living as yourself

Not integration. Participation. Including yourself while drinking tea, deciding, dating, working, resting, arguing, grieving. You stop watching your life from outside and start living it from within.

This is not a curriculum, and you won’t be handed worksheets. It’s a held, private space with a clear direction, shaped around your life.

What I won’t promise you

That you’ll become unshakably confident.

Confidence comes from movement, from doing. What changes is this: you’ll have enough of yourself to keep moving.

That your life will look different from the outside.

Maybe it will. Maybe, externally, little changes. But the dynamic changes completely: you stop living your life from outside of it. And the relationship you have with yourself quietly teaches everyone else how to treat you.

That you’ll never leave yourself again.

You will. We all do. The difference is that you’ll notice sooner, and you’ll know the way back.

Walking beside you

Nina Rose

I’ve spent years holding people through tender, honest inner work, in recovery spaces and in private one-to-one coaching. What I’ve seen, again and again, is this: most women don’t need to become someone else.

They need to stop abandoning the woman who is already there. They need support learning how to notice her, stay with her, speak from her, and include her in the life she is already living.

My work lives in the body, because knowing something isn’t the same as feeling it. Inside these four months there is parts work, embodiment, movement, pleasure, self-talk, and a lot of honest conversation. But underneath all of it is one practice: staying in relationship with yourself.

This is the most personal thing I offer. It’s just us: your life, my full attention, and four months of coming back to you.

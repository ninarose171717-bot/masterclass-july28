You know how to keep going.

You know how to show up. How to anticipate what other people need. How to keep the peace. How to be useful. How to make things work. How to hold it all together.

And maybe, in many ways, you are caring for yourself.

Maybe you journal. Maybe you move your body. Maybe you take the walks, drink the water, book the therapy, light the candle, pull the cards, create the space for you.

And still … there are moments when life is life-ing and you leave yourself behind.

A conversation gets tense, and suddenly your attention is on keeping them okay.

Someone is disappointed, and you forget what you actually feel.

You want something, but before you even let yourself have the desire, you start negotiating it down.

You feel hurt, but instead of staying with yourself while feeling that hurt, you move into explaining, fixing, hiding what you really mean, performing you’re okay, or becoming very, very reasonable.

It happens because somewhere along the way, something in you learned that being loved meant being useful. Being safe meant being low-maintenance. Being connected meant not needing too much. Being okay meant leaving yourself before anyone else could. And eventually, your body starts to speak. Through exhaustion after work. Through resentment toward your partner or friends. Through restlessness and constantly planning the next thing. Through numbness in conversations you wish you could feel present for. Through cravings, shutdown, overthinking, or the ache of knowing your life looks ‘okay,’ but you don’t feel fully here for it.

This masterclass is not about blaming yourself for the ways you learned to survive. It’s about beginning to notice the moment you leave yourself … and learning how to stay with more softness towards yourself.

01

The attention pattern

Why your focus keeps moving toward what needs to be done, who needs you, how others feel, and what might go wrong, while the woman actually living your life disappears from your own attention.

02

The hidden association

Why paying attention to you may feel selfish, unsafe, inconvenient, indulgent, unfamiliar, or even dangerous. We’ll explore why your needs, desires, emotions, and inner signals may not feel easy to stay with, especially when love, conflict, fear, disappointment, or other people are involved.

03

The return

A simple way to begin staying with yourself in real time. Not by blowing up your life, becoming someone else, or adding another complicated self-care routine to your list. But by learning how to bring your attention back to the one who is feeling, needing, wanting, aching, longing, and living this life.

Bonus Practice

staying with yourself, even when you speak

You’ll also receive a simple communication practice to support you in expressing yourself without abandoning yourself. So you can begin speaking from steadiness instead of from the tender part of you that feels scared, rejected, unseen, or urgent. Not to control how someone receives you. But so that even if they don’t understand right away, you don’t have to leave yourself in order to be heard.

By the end, you’ll understand yourself differently.
Not as someone who needs to be fixed.
But as someone who is ready to stop leaving herself
in order to be loved, useful, approved of, needed, or okay.

You’ll begin to see that the quality of the relationship
you have with yourself changes the way you move
through every other relationship.

When you have your own back, you don’t have to fight so hard to be approved of.
You don’t have to prove your needs are reasonable before you’re allowed to have them.
You don’t have to hand your center over to someone else’s reaction.

You can communicate more cleanly. You can want what you want more honestly.
You can feel what you feel without making it wrong.
You can stay close to yourself, even when life pulls hard.

What you’ll walk away with

  • A deeper understanding of why you leave yourself, even when you know better. You’ll begin to see the pattern without shaming yourself for it.
  • A felt sense of what it means to stay with yourself. Not as an idea or another thing to get right, but as a practice you can return to in ordinary, real-life moments.
  • Language for the places where you over-function, people-please, numb, shrink, perform, or become “fine”. So you can recognize what’s happening while it’s happening.
  • A simple way to return to yourself in real time. Especially when you feel activated, urgent, afraid, rejected, unseen, or pulled into someone else’s emotional world.
  • A beginning point for relating to your needs, desires, and emotions differently. So the woman who is yearning, longing, needing, wanting, and feeling does not have to be abandoned by you anymore.
  • A gentle communication practice for staying connected to yourself while expressing what is true. So you can speak from steadiness, not from the part of you that feels like she has to prove, defend, collapse, or disappear.

I am here too.

I created this masterclass for the woman who looks like she’s holding it all together, but privately knows she has been living from “should” for too long.

For the woman who somewhere along the way learned that being loved meant being useful. Being safe meant being low-maintenance. Being good meant not needing too much.

In the past few years, I’ve worked with women online and in person, as well as with people in recovery in rehab, around one pattern that sits beneath so much of what keeps us stuck: the habit of leaving ourselves.

How do we stay with ourselves when life becomes difficult?

My work is grounded in the body, because knowing something isn’t the same as feeling ourselves in it. More than anything, what I bring to this work is the practice of staying with ourselves, without rushing to fix, change, or numb.

I’m originally from Slovenia and now live in Thailand. Choosing a life that felt true, rather than one that only looked right, has shaped everything about the work I do.

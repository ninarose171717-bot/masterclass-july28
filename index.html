Save My Seat

You’re doing what needs to be done.

Caring for the people you love. Building the life you once hoped for.

And yet, something quietly tells you you’re not fully here for it.

Not because you wouldn’t be happy. Somewhere along the way, your attention grew so practiced at tending everyone else’s needs that you slowly stopped including yourself in your own life.

This masterclass is an invitation to notice ourselves again, and discover what becomes possible when we do.

When Life Asks Something of Us

We stay close to ourselves, …

until something meaningful happens.

A hard conversation.

Someone pulling away.

A desire we’re not sure we’re allowed to want.

Grief.

Shame.

Too much feeling, all at once.

And almost without noticing, our attention leaves us, moving toward managing the other person, shrinking ourselves, explaining, or simply waiting it out until we can perform okay.

This is deeply human. Many of us learned that staying connected to others meant quietly leaving ourselves, not because anyone asked, but because we learned what helped us belong, and kept doing it.

Over time, life doesn’t turn bad.

It just goes flatter.

Less vivid.

Less felt.

But then … a rare afternoon walking home, or swimming in the sea … colors sharpen, the sun warms your skin, time slows. Nothing extraordinary happened. Life just feels astonishingly intimate.

I don’t think that’s life cooperating.
I think that’s us, finally staying.

What This Masterclass Is Really About

Maybe the question isn’t how to get better at self-care.
Maybe it’s: how do I stay connected to myself
while actually living my life?

Staying with yourself means you don’t have to disappear,

even mid-uncertainty, conflict, longing, or joy.

You get to remain there too,

while you love, speak, ache, long, grieve, live.

Maybe we haven’t lost ourselves at all.

Maybe we’ve simply forgotten what it feels like to stay,

while disappointed,

while afraid,

while desiring something,

while misunderstood,

while life asks us to feel.
